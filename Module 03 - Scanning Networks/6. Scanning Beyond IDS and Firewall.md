## 1. What Does "Scanning Beyond IDS/Firewall" Mean?

In the scanning phase, attackers frequently encounter defensive controls:

| Defense | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| **Firewall** | Filters packets based on rules â€” blocks unauthorized traffic |
| **IDS** | Detects suspicious patterns â€” logs and alerts |
| **IPS** | Detects *and* actively blocks attacks in real time |

These controls can:
- Block probe packets entirely
- Detect and alert on scan activity
- Log attacker IP addresses and actions
- Rate-limit or throttle connection attempts

**Evasion** is the art of modifying scanning techniques to collect the same intelligence while **avoiding triggering these controls**.

> ğŸ§  **Mental Model:**
> - Normal Scanning â†’ *"Find open doors"*
> - Evasion Scanning â†’ *"Find open doors without triggering the alarm"*

## 2. IDS vs. Firewall vs. IPS â€” The Key Differences

This distinction is **exam-critical** and frequently tested in CEH.

| Device | Primary Role | Action Taken | Traffic Path |
|--------|-------------|:------------:|:------------:|

| **IPS** | Detect and respond | Block + Alert | Inline â€” traffic passes through |

> ğŸ¯ **CEH Memory Rule:**

> - **IDS** â†’ *Security Camera* â€” watches and records, doesn't stop

### IDS Detection Methods

## 9. Timing-Based Evasion

### ğŸ“Œ Concept

**IDS systems detect scans by recognizing rapid connection bursts** â€” many probes in a short time window. By slowing down the scan, the attacker keeps probe rates below IDS detection thresholds, blending into normal background traffic.
