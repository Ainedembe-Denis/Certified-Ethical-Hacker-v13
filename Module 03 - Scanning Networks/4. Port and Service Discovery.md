## 1. What is Port and Service Discovery?

**Port and service discovery** is the process of probing a live target to identify:

| What Is Discovered | Why It Matters |
|--------------------|----------------|
| **Open ports** | Active communication channels â€” potential entry points |
| **Closed ports** | Port accessible but no service listening |
| **Filtered ports** | Firewall or ACL blocking the probe |
| **Running services** | What application is behind the port |
| **Service versions** | Exact version numbers for CVE matching |

Performed **after host discovery**, port scanning reveals the accessible attack surface of a confirmed live system.

> ğŸ§  **Mental Model:**
> - Host Discovery â†’ *"Who is alive?"*
> - Port Discovery â†’ *"What doors are open â€” and what's behind them?"*

### Why This Phase Is Critical to Attackers

| Goal | How Port Discovery Helps |
|------|--------------------------|
| Find exposed services | Reveals what's listening on which ports |
| Detect unnecessary open ports | Highlights misconfigurations |
| Find vulnerable applications | Service versions map directly to CVEs |
| Verify firewall policy | Filtered vs. open shows what security controls exist |
| Prepare for exploitation | Open + versioned = ready-to-exploit |

## 3. Understanding Port States

Nmap classifies every port it probes into one of six states. Correctly interpreting these is fundamental to the CEH exam.

| Port State | Meaning | What It Tells the Attacker |
|:----------:|---------|---------------------------|
